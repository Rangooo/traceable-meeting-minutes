# Cross-validation channel selection

Match evidence to the claim instead of applying a universal source ranking. Evaluate six dimensions:

1. **Proximity**: Did the source directly record, produce, or observe the fact?
2. **Authority**: Is it entitled or qualified to confirm this decision, metric, or rule?
3. **Independence**: Are multiple sources genuinely independent rather than repeating one upstream claim?
4. **Recency**: Does the evidence cover the time discussed in the meeting?
5. **Method transparency**: Are definitions, samples, calculations, and revisions visible?
6. **Conflicts of interest**: Does the source have incentives to selectively disclose or exaggerate?

## Select by claim type

| Claim | Preferred channels | Supporting channels | Common trap |
|---|---|---|---|
| What was said | Original audio/video, transcript, meeting chat | Multiple participant confirmations, contemporaneous notes | Treating edited minutes as verbatim speech |
| Whether a decision was made | Decision-maker confirmation, resolution, approval, decision log | Original meeting record, subsequent execution document | “Discussed” is not “decided” |
| Action ownership or progress | Owner confirmation, ticket/project system | Email, calendar, deliverable, code/document history | Stale status or a merely mentioned person |
| Internal operating metric | Data warehouse, finance/business system, metric owner | Dashboard, audit trail, sampled source records | Different refresh times or metric definitions |
| Corporate financial claim | Audited statements, regulatory filing, exchange record, IR material | Earnings call, reliable financial database or media | GAAP/non-GAAP, reported/organic, quarter/run-rate mismatch |
| Legal or regulatory requirement | Effective statute, official gazette, regulator rule, judgment | Regulator guidance, professional database, legal counsel | Draft treated as law; wrong jurisdiction or date |
| Product or technical behavior | Reproducible test, source code, official versioned docs | Standard, benchmark, independent technical analysis | Documentation and deployed versions differ |
| Scientific or medical claim | Peer-reviewed study, systematic review, authoritative guideline, original data | Professional body, preregistration, replication | One study or correlation presented as causation |
| Market size or industry trend | Method-transparent statistics, industry database, original research | Independent reports, company disclosures, specialist media | Comparing incompatible definitions or samples |
| Customer experience or sentiment | Interviews, tickets, surveys, reviews, community discussion | Support data, retention/conversion behavior | Platform sampling bias; volume is not prevalence |
| News or public event | Participant statement, on-the-scene record, government/institution record | Multiple independent reliable outlets, fact-checkers | Many outlets using one anonymous source |

## Combine evidence

- For high-impact conclusions, seek a direct record, an authorized confirmation, and independent evidence when practical.
- Count syndicated copies as one source and identify the shared upstream source.
- Resolve conflicts by comparing time, definition, coverage, and revision—not by majority vote.
- If the best channel is unavailable, state the limitation and identify who, which system, or what artifact should be checked next.
- Participant confirmation is external communication. Do not contact anyone without explicit user authorization.
